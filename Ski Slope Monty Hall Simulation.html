🏔️ About the Monty Hall Problem - Ski Lift Edition!

The Ski Lift Scenario: You're at the base of the mountain with three ski lifts. Two lifts take you to icy runs 🧊 (bad!), and one lift takes you to an amazing powder run ❄️✨ (good!). You choose a lift, then the ski patrol (who knows which lifts go where) reveals one of the OTHER lifts that goes to an icy run. You now have a choice: stick with your original lift or switch to the other unopened lift.

The Surprising Math: Your intuition might say it's 50/50, but switching lifts actually gives you a 66.7% chance of getting the powder run, while staying gives you only a 33.3% chance!

Watch the Simulation: Notice how the win rates fluctuate wildly at first (due to small sample size), but as more trials run, they converge toward the theoretical probabilities. This demonstrates the Law of Large Numbers in action! ❄️
